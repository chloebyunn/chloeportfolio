Web Developer Inter, Fall 2019

Work

As a QA engineer, I was responsible for thoroughly ensuring that my given side-branch was defect-free, and worked well alongside other developing or developed branches with the current and previous release. I clearly identified numerous defects using Jira, Bash, and several VMWares in a timely manner, while also completing sanity and regression testing.

My Work

While ensuring the current release’s stability is crucial, managing legacy test cases and creating updated test cases are future investments pieces that will be invaluable soon as the product grows. I maintained portions of Veeva Network’s large test case bank, hosted through TestRail.

Another important aspect of testing at Veeva included, understanding the user requirements and testing in the point of the end-user, and not only testing for outliers and missed edge cases. Thinking in the point of the user and how this product would be used in a real-life basis allowed me to discover different testing routes and appreciate the work I was doing.

Lastly, I participated in a user study when they were gathering feedback for a redesign of a specific page. Thinking in the point of an internal engineer and possible client, I made remarks and asked questions related to accessibility, efficiency, and practicality.

My Takeaway

I learned the one truly grows to like their work when they can appreciate how the product is going to impact real customers and their lives.
